Project Overview

This application is designed to visualize and analyze collision reports involving autonomous vehicles (AVs) in California. As autonomous vehicle technology advances and pilot programs expand, transparency and public awareness of safety incidents are crucial.

The AV Collision Tracker provides an interactive map to explore where incidents occur, filters to slice the data by manufacturer or severity, and an analytics dashboard to understand broader trends.

Data Source

The data presented in this application is sourced from the California Department of Motor Vehicles (DMV) Autonomous Vehicle Collision Reports.

Manufacturers testing autonomous vehicles on public roads in California are required to report any collision result in property damage, bodily injury, or death within 10 days of the incident.

Disclaimer: This is a student project for educational purposes. The location data is geocoded based on description and may have inaccuracies. Please refer to official DMV reports for official use.

Features

  • Interactive Map: View exact locations of reported collisions.
  • Smart Filtering: Filter by vehicle manufacturer, collision type, control state, and severity.
  • Analytics Dashboard: Visualize trends over time and compare manufacturer safety records.
