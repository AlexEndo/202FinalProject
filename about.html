Project Overview

Autonomous vehicles represent one of the most transformative technologies of our time, promising to revolutionize transportation, reduce accidents caused by human error, and improve mobility for millions. However, as with any emerging technology, the path to widespread adoption requires rigorous testing, transparent reporting, and public scrutiny. California has positioned itself as a global leader in autonomous vehicle development, with numerous companies testing self-driving cars on public roads throughout the state. With this innovation comes responsibilityâ€”manufacturers are required to report every collision involving their autonomous vehicles to ensure accountability and track safety performance.

The AV Collision Tracker was created to make this critical safety data accessible and understandable to the public. While collision reports are publicly available through the California Department of Motor Vehicles, they exist primarily as individual PDF documents that are difficult to analyze collectively. This application transforms that raw data into an interactive, visual experience that enables researchers, policymakers, journalists, and concerned citizens to explore patterns, identify trends, and gain insights into autonomous vehicle safety across the state.

Through an interactive map interface, users can see exactly where collisions have occurred, examine details about each incident, and filter by multiple criteria including manufacturer, collision type, vehicle control state, and severity. The analytics dashboard provides temporal trends and comparative statistics that reveal how different manufacturers perform over time. By democratizing access to this information, this project aims to foster informed public discourse about autonomous vehicle safety and support evidence-based decision-making as this technology continues to evolve.

Data Source

The data presented in this application is sourced from the California Department of Motor Vehicles (DMV) Autonomous Vehicle Collision Reports.

Manufacturers testing autonomous vehicles on public roads in California are required to report any collision result in property damage, bodily injury, or death within 10 days of the incident.

Disclaimer: This is a student project for educational purposes. The location data is geocoded based on description and may have inaccuracies. Please refer to official DMV reports for official use.

Features

  • Interactive Map: View exact locations of reported collisions.
  • Smart Filtering: Filter by vehicle manufacturer, collision type, control state, and severity.
  • Analytics Dashboard: Visualize trends over time and compare manufacturer safety records.
